Threat & Detection Doctrine
SECRET // NOFORN 02 / 05
Why do small drones defeat conventional systems, and how is LKM-Drone different?

Small, low-cost drones fly low, move unpredictably, and generate minimal radar signature. At operational range a drone may occupy a handful of pixels — indistinguishable from background noise for conventional object recognition. The decisive question is not whether something looks like a drone, but whether it behaves like one.

FIELD-TRIAL · NIGHT EXERCISECAM-12 · SECTOR NORTH

Plate 1 — Counter-UAS field exercise. A single operator runs LKM-Drone against live airspace using a tripod thermal observation head; classification and tracking are surfaced on the tactical tablet. No dedicated radar is present.
  • Behavior over shape
    Instead of searching for a drone silhouette, Ghost evaluates motion, hovering bursts and course corrections across time.
  • Temporal, not single-frame
    A contact is judged on its trajectory and persistence — not a single ambiguous frame.
  • Discrimination by default
    Birds, debris and thermal noise are separated from stabilised flight before an alert is ever raised.
