Start a conversation
01 / 09
What do you see at the main gate right now?
01 // Start a conversation

Ask your camera anything — just talk to it like you'd talk to a colleague.

Open a new chat and type a question. There are no query builders, no rule trees, no menus to learn. Ghost looks at the live picture, understands what is happening, and writes back the answer in plain words.

Every conversation is the front door to the whole platform: the same plain-language box that answers "what do you see?" is the one you'll later use to set watch rules, scan a scene, or question the past.

How it works
  • 1
    Open a new chat
    Press the + button to start a fresh conversation.
  • 2
    Type your question
    Write it at the bottom, e.g. "What do you see at the main gate?"
  • 3
    Read the answer
    Ghost reads the live frame and writes back what it understands.

Connect cameras
02 / 09
Can this chat watch the loading bay camera?
02 // Connect cameras

Plug a camera into the chat — one feed, or many, live or saved.

A conversation can watch through a single camera or several at once: the phone in your hand right now (live), or cameras you saved earlier (fixed RTSP / HDMI feeds). Whatever they see, you can ask about — the feed appears above the chat and every question becomes about that view.

Live
Use the device camera in front of you, right now.
Saved
Reconnect a fixed camera you configured before.
How it works
  • 1
    Open the camera menu
    Pick which camera this conversation should watch.
  • 2
    Choose live or saved
    Watch right now, or reconnect a saved camera.
  • 3
    Ask away
    The feed appears above the chat — your questions are now about what it sees.

Organize
03 / 09
How do I keep 40 cameras from turning into chaos?
03 // Organize

Sort cameras into areas and groups — tidy boxes inside tidy boxes.

When you run many cameras, you file them. An Area is a big box (like "North Yard"). Inside it you make Groups (like "Gates"). Then you drag each camera-chat into the right box, so the sidebar mirrors the real site instead of an endless flat list.

This structure isn't just cosmetic: areas and groups are also what let you later question a whole zone at once (see capability 08).

How it works
  • 1
    Add an area
    Press "Add area" and name your big zone.
  • 2
    Add a group
    Inside the area, press + to add a group.
  • 3
    Drag to file
    Drag a conversation into the area or group to file it there.

System prompt
04 / 09
You guard the parking lot. Tell me about anyone near the cars.
04 // System prompt

Tell Ghost what to care about — a standing brief for this camera.

A system prompt is a short note you give Ghost so it knows its job for this chat — like "You guard the parking lot. Tell me about anyone near the cars." It carries that focus into every answer, so you don't have to repeat yourself.

Each conversation keeps its own brief, which means the same platform can act as a gate guard in one chat and a loading-dock supervisor in another.

How it works
  • 1
    Open chat settings
    Tap the sliders icon for this conversation.
  • 2
    Write the focus
    Describe what this camera should pay attention to.
  • 3
    Save
    Now every answer follows your instructions.

Memory
05 / 09
What have you logged on this camera so far?
05 // Memory

Ghost remembers what it saw — a notebook you can open any time.

Ghost keeps a small notebook for each chat. Tracking lists every person and vehicle it spotted. Observations are short notes about them. Facts are the things it learned and should keep remembering. Open the notebook whenever you want to read it back.

Tracking
Every person and vehicle Ghost noticed.
Observations
Short, timestamped notes about them.
Facts
Durable knowledge worth remembering.
Always readable
Open the panel at any moment.
How it works
  • 1
    Open the memory panel
    Tap the brain icon.
  • 2
    Turn on tracking
    Ghost logs people and vehicles as it sees them.
  • 3
    Read it back
    Switch tabs for observations and saved facts.

Scan the scene
06 / 09
Give me a full read of everything in this frame.
06 // Scan the scene

One button to read the whole place — a quick safety check of the room.

Tap the scan button and Ghost takes one good look at everything in the picture, then writes a full report: what's normal, and what looks off. Instead of asking one question at a time, you get a structured sweep of the entire scene in a single pass.

Use it when you walk up to a feed cold and want the whole story before you start asking follow-ups.

How it works
  • 1
    Use a saved camera
    Make sure the chat is connected to a camera.
  • 2
    Press scan
    Tap the eye button in the header.
  • 3
    Read the report
    Ghost writes a full account of the whole scene.

Ask about the past
07 / 09
Who passed the loading bay last night?
07 // Ask about the past

Who was here? What happened? — ask, don't scrub.

Ghost doesn't forget. You can ask about earlier today or last night — "Who passed the loading bay?" — and it answers from what it actually saw and wrote down, with the time. No scrubbing through hours of footage; the feed has become searchable memory.

Because answers come from timestamped observations, you get the "who / what / when" directly instead of a clip to review.

How it works
  • 1
    Open a watched chat
    Any conversation that has watched a camera.
  • 2
    Ask about the past
    A "who / what / when" question about earlier.
  • 3
    Get a timestamped answer
    Ghost replies from its saved observations and times.

Talk to a whole zone
08 / 09
Is anyone in the north yard right now?
08 // Talk to a whole zone

Ask many cameras at once — one question, the whole zone answers.

Click an area or group name and you talk to all of its cameras together. Ask once — "Is anyone in the yard?" — and Ghost checks every camera in that zone and answers as one, instead of you hopping between feeds.

This is where the structure from capability 03 pays off: a tidy area is also a single thing you can question.

How it works
  • 1
    Select a zone
    Click an area or group name (or the radio icon).
  • 2
    Ask once
    Type a single question for the whole zone.
  • 3
    Get one answer
    Ghost looks through every camera there and replies as one.

Alerts
09 / 09
Tell me if the gate opens after 10pm.
09 // Alerts

Give each chat its own watch rules — Ghost watches so you don't have to.

For every camera-chat you can write rules in plain words, like "Tell me if the gate opens after 10pm." Turn alert mode on and Ghost watches by itself, staying quiet until your rule actually happens — then it speaks up.

Rules live per conversation, so each camera enforces exactly the watch that matters for its spot.

How it works
  • 1
    Open the alert panel
    Tap the shield icon.
  • 2
    Write a rule
    Describe the watch in plain words and turn it on.
  • 3
    Flip alert mode on
    Ghost watches on its own and alerts you when it triggers.
